Your first visit
What happens at my first appointment?

Your first visit starts with a skin consultation — Alex will take time to learn about your skin history, current routine, concerns, and goals before touching your face. This isn't a quick intake form; it's a real conversation.

From there, Alex will assess your skin and either begin a treatment that day or recommend the best starting point based on what she finds. You'll leave with a clear understanding of your skin type, what's going on beneath the surface, and a plan for where to go next.

New clients are encouraged to book the free consultation first so Alex can get to know your skin before recommending a service.
How should I prepare for my appointment?

Come with a clean or lightly moisturized face if possible — no heavy makeup. If you're coming straight from work or your day, that's completely fine too.

Let Alex know about any new products you've started recently, any medications you're taking (especially retinoids or prescription acne treatments), and any recent skin reactions. The more she knows going in, the more she can tailor the treatment.

Avoid the following in the 48 hours before your appointment: exfoliating scrubs, retinol or retinoids, chemical exfoliants (AHAs/BHAs), waxing or threading on the face, and any spray tans.

What should I avoid after a facial?

For the first 24–48 hours after your facial, give your skin time to settle. Avoid direct sun exposure and wear SPF if you're heading outside. Skip active ingredients like retinol, exfoliating acids, and vitamin C serums for at least 24 hours.

Avoid heavy workouts, steam rooms, or anything that causes a lot of sweating right after your appointment — your skin is more receptive than usual and you want to keep it calm.

Alex will give you specific post-care guidance based on the treatment you received, so if you're ever unsure just follow her recommendations over general advice.

I have sensitive skin — is that okay?

Absolutely. Sensitive and reactive skin is something Alex works with regularly — the Barrier Repair Facial is specifically designed for skin that needs a gentler, restorative approach.

Just let Alex know about your sensitivities when you book and during your consultation. She'll adjust every step of the treatment accordingly and avoid anything that could trigger a reaction.

Acne & Face Reality
Why do I have to use Face Reality products?

The Face Reality protocol works because what happens at home is just as important as what happens in the treatment room. The products Alex uses during your facial are formulated to work together with your home routine — using other products in between sessions can slow your progress or trigger breakouts that make it harder to track what's working.

Think of it like a prescription — the treatment only works as intended when all the pieces are followed. Alex will explain every product she recommends, what it does, and why it's in your routine. Nothing goes in your bag without a reason.

The product requirement applies to clients enrolled in acne boot camp. For one-off Acne-Safe Reset Facials, Alex can work with what you have and make recommendations from there.
Are Face Reality products expensive?

Face Reality products are mid-range in price — comparable to what you'd spend at Sephora on skincare, but specifically chosen for acne-prone skin and formulated to work within the protocol. Most clients find they actually spend less overall once they stop buying products that don't work.

Alex will build you a routine with only what you actually need — no unnecessary steps, no products just to fill a shelf. The goal is a lean, effective routine that gets results.

What is the Acne Boot Camp and who is it for?

The Acne Boot Camp is a 4-session clear skin program built around the Face Reality method. It's designed for anyone dealing with persistent acne who's ready to commit to a real plan — not just a single facial.

It's a good fit if you've tried a lot of products without lasting results, if your acne comes back every time you stop treating it, or if you've never had a proper skin analysis and want to understand what's actually driving your breakouts.

Sessions are spaced 2–4 weeks apart. In between, Alex checks in to adjust your home routine as your skin responds. The Face Reality product routine is required for boot camp clients.

Can I book an acne treatment if my acne is mild?

Yes — you don't need to be dealing with severe acne to benefit from an acne-safe approach. Even mild or occasional breakouts often respond well to the Face Reality method, and catching patterns early means you can stay ahead of them.

If you're unsure whether acne treatments or a customized facial is the better starting point, book a free consultation and Alex will point you in the right direction.

Results & timelines
How many sessions until I see results?

It depends on what you're treating. For general skin health, hydration, and glow — most clients notice a difference after their first or second session.

For acne, the honest answer is that real, lasting results take 3–6 months of consistent treatment and a supported home routine. Some clients see significant clearing within the first few sessions. Others go through an adjustment period where things look a little worse before they get better — this is normal and expected as congestion works its way to the surface.

The most important thing is consistency. Skipping sessions or going off your home routine in between sets the process back. Alex tracks your progress at every visit and adjusts the plan as needed.

Will my skin get worse before it gets better?

For some acne clients, yes — and this is actually a sign the treatment is working. When you start clearing congestion that's been sitting under the skin, it can come to the surface before it clears. This is called purging and it's a normal part of the process.

Alex will prepare you for what to expect and will monitor your skin closely throughout. If something doesn't look right or feels outside of normal, she'll adjust your routine. You're never left to figure it out alone.

How often should I come in?

For general skin maintenance — once a month is the sweet spot for most people. Your skin naturally turns over roughly every 28 days, so monthly facials align with that cycle and keep things consistently on track.

For acne boot camp — sessions are spaced every 2–4 weeks depending on how your skin is responding. Alex will let you know the ideal timing at each visit.

For barrier repair or sensitive skin — frequency depends on where your skin is at. Alex will recommend a cadence that makes sense for your specific situation.
