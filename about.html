The acne journey

I know what it feels like to
dread the mirror

Growing up on the Central Coast, I struggled with acne through my teenage years. And if you've been there, you know it's never just about the skin. It's the way you hold your hair to cover a breakout. The photos you avoid. The way a bad skin day can set the tone for everything else.

I tried everything — products from every shelf, advice from every corner of the internet. Some things helped a little. Most things didn't. What I was missing wasn't a new product. It was someone who could actually explain what was happening in my skin and build a real plan around it.

That experience never left me. It's the reason I pursued the Face Reality certification — because their protocol is built on exactly the kind of consistent, educated, methodical care that I wish someone had given me. And it's the reason every acne client I see feels like a personal mission.

Why esthetics

Skin is where science
and confidence meet

I've always been drawn to work that's both technical and deeply human. Esthetics sits right at that intersection — there's real science behind what we do, clinical ingredients with measurable effects, protocols built on research. But the outcome isn't just clearer skin. It's someone walking out feeling more like themselves.

I enrolled at the Design School of Cosmetology in Paso Robles, graduated, and got my license — and it felt like the beginning of something I'd been building toward for a long time.

From there, the Face Reality certification was a natural next step. Acne is what I understand most personally, and their method — grounded in consistency, the right ingredients, and real client education — lines up exactly with how I believe skin care should work.

How I work

Three things every
client can count on

01
You'll always know why
I don't just perform treatments — I explain them. Every product, every step, every recommendation comes with a reason. You leave each appointment understanding your skin better than when you walked in.
02
I meet your skin where it is
No two sessions are the same because no two skin days are the same. I assess what's actually happening before every treatment and adjust accordingly — not just follow a set menu.
03
This is a long game
Real skin change takes time and consistency. I'm not interested in one-off fixes. I want to know your skin over months and years, track your progress, and be the person you call when something changes.
